Home Credit Default Risk

I took KDD as my summer course and took part in Kaggle competition as a project.

Link to Competition: Home Credit Default

About Project

Home Credit is a consumer finance provider which focus on lending to people with little or no credit history. To make use of the data to enable Home Credit to make predictions on client's repayment abilities. This will help Home Credit to ensure that clients who can repay are not rejected, but are given a loan with a principle, maturity, and repayment calendar that the loan proves empowering to both Home Credit and the client.

What I did

Along with my team, I applied CRISP-DM process to the Kaggle dataset.

1) Understanding Business Problem: As a first step we defined the goal for the project and translated it to data analytic problem.
2) Exploratory data analysis: Since the data was huge we had to write our own wrapper libraries to perform EDA, for ex. we added library to plot barplots, histograms and boxplots based on frequency binning.
3) Data Preparation: The kaggle data had lot of imbalance between majority and minority target classes. We used ROSE library and SMOTE to reduce imbalances. After that we performed Z-score standardization for linear regression and Min-Max for XGBoost. We also reduced the dimensions of the train data from 121 to 49. After this we also joined Credit and Bureau files to train data. We also did imputation of missing data using HMISC and MICE.
4) Modelling We performed two modelling techniques:
a) Linear Regression Model - To find variables which has linear correlation with target class b) Ensemble XGBoost - Decision tree was run 100 fold and we achieved RMSE .30

Predicting Successful likes on Youtube

I took topics of Intelligent Systems as my Spring 2018 course.

About Project

Predicting successful clicks for an advertisment are critical for any organization. With rise in Social Networks it is important to know factors leading to successful click on advertisments.

What I did

Using Python NLTK package, I did text analysis - TF-IDF vectorization and Sentiment Analysis

1) Data scrapping: Using Beautiful Soup, I scrapped data from the youtube channel.
2) Preprocessing: Removing tags, frequent words and creation of POS tagging tree.
3) Analytics: Using Wordnet to get words with weights on Sentiments. Profanity words and Synonymns. We created chain of words often used together. We also tried Bigram and Trigram.
4) Modelling We performed followin modelling techniques:
a) Support Vector Machine b) Random Forest c) Bigram and Trigram Decision Trees.
